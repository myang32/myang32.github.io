Ace Attorney: Cross-cultural allusions

A user-centered libguide of historical and mythical references in the Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney Trilogy.

Project Description

In groups, design and develop an informative LibGuide for a target audience regarding a specific topic. Compile relevant resources for research, incorporating diverse types of content such as databases, e-books, journal articles, and multimedia.

Methods

We were first divided into groups by the instructor based on our interests. From there, we brainstormed and discussed our topic, divided our roles and sections, and carried out various parts of the LibGuide creation with thorough communication and an agreed timeline. Other than the completed LibGuide, we completed a final report that was critically reflective of our project, and shared the LibGuide with our peers through a group presentation in class.

Role

Micaela: Project manager, ensuring that progress and regular communication is made each week. She also wrote the homepage text and the Historical and Mythical Reference page. She worked on this project with Grace Croken and Alice Cheng.

Learning Outcome Achieved: Foundations of Library & Information Studies

Rationale

This project demonstrates foundations of library and information studies by applying core concepts and theories through collecting, organizing, and providing access to information in the form of a guide. The guide is designed to be a point of reference, providing useful sources across various formats, all of which are carefully assessed for their relevance and reliability. Following ACRL frameworks, the guide is also instructional with its integration of tutorial videos, resources, and research guidance. With strong understanding of the goals of reference and instruction, the guide ensures that all users are able to engage with the content regardless of their understanding of the topic.

Learning Outcome Achieved: User-centered services

Rationale

This project demonstrates user-centered services by addressing the information needs of different user communities, including university students, faculty, researchers and casual gamers. This guide is designed to support both academic research and personal exploration into Visual Novels and the Ace Attorney video game trilogy featuring a selection of interdisciplinary resources including scholarly articles, literature, videos and interactive content. These materials explore the games cultural, mythological and literary allusions, while also providing insight and information into the origins of visual novels, free-to-play examples, guidance on how to create your own and further reading on the genre. While the guide may be best suited for individuals with a background in video game studies, media studies, narrative design, or creative writing, the guide was created so that anyone can interact with it in an accessible and meaningful way.
